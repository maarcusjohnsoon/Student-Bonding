Results Guide

Understanding your score and fostering meaningful conversations

Your Rice Purity Test score is just the beginning. What matters most is how you use this information to connect with others, reflect on your journey, and build meaningful relationships based on understanding and respect.

Interpreting Your Score

80-100 High Purity

You've had fewer experiences in the areas the test covers. This often reflects focused priorities, strong personal values, or simply being early in your journey of exploration.

Conversation Starter: "What experiences are you most curious about, and what holds you back or motivates you to wait?"
50-79 Moderate Experience

You've had a balanced mix of experiences. This suggests you're actively exploring life while maintaining some boundaries and making thoughtful choices.

Conversation Starter: "What experiences have shaped your perspective the most, and how do you decide what's worth trying?"
20-49 Broad Experience

You've had many varied experiences across different areas. This often indicates openness to new things and comfort with exploration and experimentation.

Conversation Starter: "What's the most valuable lesson you've learned from your experiences, and what would you do differently?"
0-19 Extensive Experience

You've experienced a lot across many categories. This might reflect a very adventurous spirit, or it could indicate areas where you might benefit from reflection on your choices.

Conversation Starter: "How do you balance adventure with safety and self-care in your life?"

Conversation Starters

Use these prompts to spark meaningful discussions with friends, regardless of your scores:

"What's one experience you're glad you tried, and why?"

"How do you decide what's worth experimenting with in college?"

"What's something you thought you wanted to try but changed your mind about?"

"How do you handle peer pressure when it conflicts with your values?"

"What's the most important lesson you've learned about relationships?"

"How has your perspective on risk-taking changed since starting college?"

"What's one boundary you're proud of maintaining?"

"How do you balance being open to new experiences with staying safe?"

"What role do your friends play in your decision-making?"

"What's something you wish you'd known before college about relationships?"

"How do you define a meaningful connection with someone?"

"What's the best advice you'd give to someone just starting college?"

How to Keep Discussions Respectful

  • Listen without judgment: Everyone's journey is different and valid
  • Avoid pressure: Never push someone to share more than they're comfortable with
  • Respect boundaries: If someone doesn't want to discuss something, move on gracefully
  • Share thoughtfully: Consider how your words might affect others
  • Focus on growth: Frame conversations around learning and supporting each other
  • Maintain privacy: Don't share others' results or stories without permission
  • Be supportive: Offer encouragement rather than criticism
  • Stay present: Put away phones and distractions during meaningful conversations

Conversation Pact

Copy this agreement to share with your group before starting discussions:

STUDENT BONDING CONVERSATION PACT

We agree to:
• Respect each other's privacy and boundaries
• Listen without judgment or criticism
• Keep all shared information confidential
• Support each other's growth and well-being
• Speak only for ourselves and our own experiences
• Create a safe space for honest, open dialogue
• Remember that everyone's journey is unique and valid
• Focus on connection rather than comparison

By participating, we commit to making this a positive, 
meaningful experience for everyone involved.
